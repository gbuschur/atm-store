Why it exists

Every aircraft transition has the same hidden risk: no one is working from the same truth.

Findings live in emails. Records questions live in side channels. Inspection notes become PDFs too early. Decisions get made without a complete view of technical, records, conformity, and commercial exposure.

01

Fragmented findings

Physical inspection, records review, conformity, and open items are separated when they should be connected.

02

Unclear ownership

Issues move across lessor, buyer, inspector, and MRO teams without a controlled assignment path.

03

Late surprises

Material exposure often appears after the inspection, when the deal clock is already moving.

04

Weak audit trail

Static reports capture a moment. ATM preserves the decision path that got the aircraft there.

Not another inspection tool. A transaction control layer.

ATM is built to sit between the parties during a live aircraft transition, keeping the work structured, visible, and accountable until the aircraft is ready for the next phase.

Operator-led

Built from real transition work, not software theory.

The platform is designed around narrowbody aircraft transition realities: physical inspection, technical records, AD/STC status, engine/APU/landing gear records, open items, FAA conformity, phase readiness, and final report generation.
