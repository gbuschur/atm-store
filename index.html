Why it exists

Every aircraft transition has the same hidden risk: no one is working from the same truth.

Findings live in emails. Records questions live in side channels. Inspection notes become PDFs too early. Decisions get made without a complete view of technical, records, conformity, and commercial exposure.

01

Fragmented findings

Physical inspection, records review, conformity, and open items are separated when they should be connected in one controlled view.

02

Unclear ownership

Issues move across lessor, buyer, inspector, and MRO teams without a controlled assignment path or resolution accountability.

03

Late surprises

Material exposure often surfaces after the inspection, when the deal clock is already moving and leverage is reduced.

04

Weak audit trail

Static reports capture a moment. ATM preserves the decision path, the evidence, and the resolution history behind every finding.

Not another inspection tool. A transaction control layer.

ATM sits between the parties during a live aircraft transition — keeping the work structured, visible, and accountable from first document load to final sign-off.
